Titration of a solution

Apparatus and Materials:

Standardized NaOH solution (1 mol/L), unknown HCl solution, Phenolphthalein indicator solution, distilled water. 150 mL Beaker, 100 mL Graduated Cylinder, 50 mL Volumetric Burette, 100 mL Volumetric Flask, 100 mL beaker, Universal Stand, Titration Clamp, Digital pH meter

Procedure :

1. Practice using a burette:

Transfer 100 mL of water into the 150 mL beaker using a graduated cylinder. Put it on the magnetic agitator, add the magnetic bar and turn on the agitator. Fill the 50 mL volumetric burette with distilled water until the calibration mark using a beaker. Open the tap and try to control the quantity of water that you add in the beaker.

2. Colorimetric titration:

2.1.Prepare 100 mL of sodium hydroxide solution at concentration 1M. Which glassware should we use to prepare 100 mL of a 1 M NaOH solution? Why? Note the exact value of the mass you took. Empty water of the burette, rinse and fill it with prepared NaOH solution. To rinse the burette, add 2-3 mL of NaOH solution and allow it to run through the burette. Repeat 3 times.
2.2. Take the 100 mL volumetric flask, add roughly 50 mL distilled water. Under the hood take 10 mL of unknown concentration hydrochloric acid with a pipette and transfer it into the 100 mL volumetric flask. Fill the flask with distilled water until the calibration mark. Transfer the solution into the 150 mL beaker. Add the magnetic bar and 3 drops of phenolphthalein indicator. Why is indicator required? What is its function?
2.3. Put the 150 mL beaker on the agitator. Add 2 mL of NaOH from the burette and observe the colour change in the beaker. Purple colour appears and then disappears in the beaker. Why the purple colour disappears? Repeat this operation many times by adding really small volumes of NaOH. When the colour stays purple you have reached the equivalence point. Estimate roughly the equivalent volume Veq and find the concentration of hydrochloric acid solution.

3. Titration using a pH-meter

3.1 Fill the burette again with NaOH. Repeat the task 2.2. Add the pH-meter electrode in the beaker. Check that the cell dives correctly in the solution.
3.2. Measure the pH value for different volumes of added NaOH. Taka a measurement after each 1 mL between V= 0 mL and V= 8 mL. Then try to take a measurement after each 0.2 mL from V= 8 mL to V= 12 mL and then a measurement after each 1mL from V= 12 mL to V= 20 mL. See the example in the table below:
